Section 101
Basic Tenant Rights
A tenant has the right to a safe and habitable living environment. The landlord must maintain the structural integrity of the premises and provide essential services like water and electricity.

Section 102
Eviction Notice Period
A landlord cannot evict a tenant arbitrarily. A minimum notice period of 30 days must be provided in writing. Eviction without notice is illegal unless there is severe property damage or non-payment of rent for consecutive months.

Section 103
Security Deposit Refund
The landlord must return the security deposit within 15 days of the tenant vacating the property. Any deductions must be itemized and justified (e.g., unpaid rent or property damage beyond normal wear and tear). Unjustified withholding is a legal offense.

Section 104
Rent Increase Limits
Rent can only be increased by a maximum of 5% annually, unless significant renovations have been agreed upon by both parties.

Section 105
Right to Privacy
A landlord cannot enter the rented premises without providing at least 24 hours of prior notice, except in cases of genuine emergencies such as fire or flooding. The tenant has the right to quiet enjoyment of the property.

Section 106
Maintenance and Repairs
The landlord is legally responsible for major repairs such as plumbing, structural, and electrical faults. If the landlord fails to make necessary repairs within a reasonable time after being notified, the tenant may repair and deduct the cost from the rent.

Section 107
Protection Against Unlawful Lockouts
It is illegal for a landlord to lock a tenant out of the rental property or shut off utilities to force an eviction. Such actions count as a "self-help" eviction and the tenant can sue for damages.

Section 108
Subletting Rules
A tenant may only sublease the property if it is explicitly permitted in the rental agreement. If the agreement is silent, the tenant must obtain written consent from the landlord before subletting.

Section 109
Anti-Discrimination Law
A landlord cannot refuse to rent based on a tenant's religion, caste, gender, marital status, or dietary preferences. Discrimination in housing is a punishable offense.

Section 110
Rent Receipts
A landlord is legally obligated to provide a duly signed rent receipt for every payment made by the tenant, whether paid in cash, check, or online.

Section 111
Death of a Tenant
In the event of the tenant's death, the tenancy rights generally pass on to the tenant's legal heirs who were living with the tenant at the time of death.

Section 112
Essential Services Continuation
A landlord cannot deliberately disconnect essential services such as water supply, electricity, or sanitary services, even if the tenant has failed to pay rent.

Section 113
Late Payment Fees
A landlord can charge late fees for delayed rent only if it is explicitly stated in the rental agreement. The fee must be reasonable and cannot be an exorbitant penalty.

Section 114
Notice to Vacate by Tenant
A tenant must typically give a 30-day written notice to the landlord before vacating the premises, unless the rental agreement specifies otherwise.

Section 115
Return of Advance Rent
If a tenant vacates the property earlier than anticipated due to landlord breach, any advance rent paid for unused months must be refunded immediately.

Section 116
Right to Visitors
A tenant has the right to host guests and visitors without unreasonable interference from the landlord, provided the guests do not cause a nuisance or violate terms of the lease.

Section 117
Lease Renewal
A landlord cannot arbitrarily refuse to renew a lease if the tenant has complied with all terms. Refusal to renew must be backed by a valid reason such as the landlord needing the property for personal use.

Section 118
Damage Documentation
A tenant has the right to conduct a joint inspection of the property with the landlord at the beginning and end of the lease to document pre-existing damages to protect their security deposit.

Section 119
Commercial Usage of Residential Property
A tenant cannot use a residential rental property for commercial or business purposes without the prior written consent of the landlord.
