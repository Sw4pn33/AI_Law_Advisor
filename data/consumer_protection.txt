Section 1
Consumer Rights Protection
Every consumer has the right to be protected against the marketing of goods and services which are hazardous to life and property. The right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard and price of goods.

Section 2
Defective Goods Refund
If a product is found defective within the warranty period, the consumer is entitled to a full refund, replacement, or repair. The seller cannot refuse a refund if the defect is reported promptly. For goods exceeding ₹1000, documentary evidence like a bill is required. Fraudulent refusal is punishable.

Section 3
Unfair Trade Practices
Selling a product above MRP, hoarding, and misleading advertisements are considered unfair trade practices. Consumers can file a complaint with the District Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission.

Section 4
Compensation for Harm
If a defective product causes physical injury or financial loss, the consumer can demand compensation from the manufacturer or seller.
