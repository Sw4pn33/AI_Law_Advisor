Section 201
Hacking and Unauthorized Access
It is a criminal offense to hack into an individual's computer, smartphone, or email account without their explicit permission. Victims can report unauthorized access to the local Cyber Crime Cell and seek compensation for damages caused.

Section 202
Identity Theft
Fraudulently using another person's electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature is punishable by law with imprisonment and a fine. Any financial loss incurred from identity theft must be reported immediately to the bank to block transactions.

Section 203
Cyberbullying and Defamation
Publishing or transmitting false or abusive content online with the intent to defame, harass, or threaten an individual is a punishable offense. The platform hosting the content may also be legally mandated to take it down immediately.

Section 301
Minimum Wage Law
Every employer must pay workers at least the legally mandated minimum wage for their state and industry. Failing to do so or delaying wages allows the employee to file a complaint with the local labor court.

Section 302
Working Hours and Overtime
An employee's standard workday cannot exceed common legal limits (typically 8 to 9 hours). Any work required beyond these limits must be compensated with overtime pay, which is usually at twice the ordinary rate of regular wages.

Section 303
Maternity Leave Benefits
A pregnant employee is entitled to significant paid maternity leave (typically 26 weeks in some jurisdictions), provided she meets the minimum employment tenure. An employer cannot unlawfully terminate an expecting mother during her pregnancy.

Section 304
Wrongful Termination
An employer cannot terminate an employee without a valid reason and proper notice periods. Severance pay or notice period compensation must be provided unless the termination is strictly for proven major misconduct or policy violation.

Section 401
Driving Without a Valid License
Any individual operating a motor vehicle must possess a valid, unexpired driving license. Driving without one, or letting an underage person drive, incurs severe fines and potential confiscation of the vehicle.

Section 402
Compensation for Road Accidents
In the event of a road accident resulting in injury or death, the victim or their family is entitled to financial compensation under "No Fault Liability" clauses, regardless of who primarily caused the accident.

Section 403
Drunk Driving Penalties
Operating a vehicle under the influence of alcohol or drugs exceeding the permissible statutory limit is a severe penal offense, leading to immediate license suspension, heavy fines, and potential jail time.

Section 501
Right to Public Information
Any citizen has the right to request information, inspect public documents, or take certified copies of records from any government authority. The public authority must respond within typically 30 days of receiving the request.

Section 502
Exemptions from Disclosure
Information that affects national security, critical trade secrets, personal privacy of other individuals, or active criminal investigations is exempt and cannot be demanded by citizens under the public information right.

Section 601
Domestic Violence Protection
Any individual facing physical, emotional, or economic abuse within a domestic relationship has the right to seek a protection order, residence rights, and compensation under domestic violence protection laws.

Section 602
Right to Maintenance
A spouse, dependent child, or elderly parent who is unable to maintain themselves financially is legally entitled to claim financial maintenance and support from a capable family member.
